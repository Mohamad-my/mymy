Medical Devices

Pressure device

A blood pressure monitor, or sphygmomanometer, is a medical device used to measure the force of blood against the walls of the arteries as the heart pumps blood. It is commonly used to diagnose high or low blood pressure and to monitor conditions like chronic hypertension. The device typically consists of several key components:

  • Cuff: This is the part that wraps around the personâ€™s arm. It is inflated with air to compress the arteries, allowing the measurement of blood pressure.
  • Manometer: The part that displays the blood pressure readings, which can either be a digital screen or an analog dial with a needle.
  • Air Pump: Used to inflate the cuff. In electronic devices, this process is automatic, while in manual devices, the user inflates the cuff using a hand pump.
  • Deflation Valve: Controls the release of air from the cuff, usually allowing for gradual deflation to obtain accurate pressure readings.

    • Types of Blood Pressure Monitors:

  • Manual (Mercury or Aneroid): These require more skill to use because the user must listen for the sound of blood flow (Korotkoff sounds) with a stethoscope while slowly releasing air from the cuff.
  • Electronic or Digital Monitors: These rely on electronic sensors to measure blood pressure and display the results directly on a digital screen. They are user-friendly and don't require a stethoscope, making them ideal for home use.
